# Research: Task-first Work Tools

## Market signal

Meeting follow-up is frequent, understandable, and costly when ownership or agreement is ambiguous. Existing AI meeting products often centre recording and summarisation. MeetingProof deliberately occupies the narrower assurance gap: exact source lines, missing-field visibility, named human review, and an exportable record.

Project change assurance and precedent retrieval are less universal but stronger professional proof. They remain adjacent tools rather than being merged into one complicated interface.

## Technology fit

LangGraph earns its place in MeetingProof because the workflow must stop and resume at a human approval gate while preserving the original state. LangSmith is useful for regression evaluation against synthetic notes and for measuring unsupported claims, missed actions, human edits, cost, and latency.

A multi-agent orchestrator is unnecessary for a single extraction and approval path. Adding one would increase operational and explanatory cost without improving the first user outcome.

## Product conclusion

Lead with the relatable task, then reveal the engineering. Publish one complete meeting workflow and one safe connector tutorial before widening the workbench.
